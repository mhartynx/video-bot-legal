Video Bot Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 5, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Video Bot handles information when used to generate short vertical videos and upload them to supported publishing platforms, including TikTok.

Information the Tool Handles

Video Bot may process video files, generated scripts, captions, metadata, audio files, subtitle timing data, topic prompts, publishing status, and platform authorization tokens. This information is used to create videos and, when authorized, upload them to the connected platform account.

TikTok Account Data

When you authorize TikTok access, TikTok may provide access tokens and limited account information allowed by the scopes you approve. Video Bot uses this information only to authenticate requests and upload videos or related post information on behalf of the authorized account.

Local Storage

Video Bot is designed to run locally or in an environment controlled by the creator. Generated files and authorization tokens may be stored on that machine or environment, such as in local output and data folders. You are responsible for protecting access to those files.

No Sale of Personal Information

Video Bot does not sell personal information. The tool does not include advertising trackers or analytics scripts in these policy pages.

Third-Party Services

The tool may send content or requests to third-party services that you configure, such as TikTok for uploads, OpenAI for script or metadata generation, text-to-speech services for voice generation, and YouTube if you enable YouTube upload functionality. Those services process information according to their own terms and privacy policies.

Data Retention

Locally generated files and tokens remain in your environment until you delete them. Uploaded content remains subject to the retention and deletion controls of the platform where it was uploaded.

Revoking Access

You can revoke TikTok access through your TikTok account settings. You may also delete locally stored token files from the machine or environment where Video Bot runs.

Security

You should keep API keys, client secrets, OAuth tokens, and generated content private. Do not publish token files, environment files, or platform credentials in public repositories.

Contact

For privacy questions, contact the project owner through the GitHub repository where this page is published.
